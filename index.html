Assessment Food Expenditure Among Syrian Refugees Households

Introduction

The Syrian crisis, which began in 2011, led to the displacement of approximately 5.5 million refugees, severely impacting their ability to meet basic needs, especially food. Food is a fundamental pillar for every household, and factors such as low income, large family sizes, and limited government support have made it increasingly difficult to cover food expenditures. These challenges have significantly influenced food choices, with potential long-term effects on health and well-being. This project aims to analyze these factors and assess their impact on food expenditure patterns in refugee households, focusing on regions in Kurdistan, Iraq, and Jordan.

Business Impact

Uncovering these insights can drive meaningful action in several key areas:

  • Supporting Host Countries: The data can assist host countries in identifying the most critical needs of refugees, enabling them to target food assistance more effectively and ensure that the most vulnerable households receive adequate support.
  • Helping Organizations Tackle Food Insecurity: Relevant organizations can leverage this data to develop strategies for addressing food insecurity, creating job opportunities for qualified refugees, and offering training programs for those not yet able to enter the labor market—aligning with the host country's regulations and economic landscape.

Tools Used

  • Excel: Used for data cleaning, basic analysis, and organizing the dataset.
  • Python: Utilized for advanced data analysis, statistical modeling, and creating predictive insights using libraries such as Pandas, NumPy, and Matplotlib.
  • Tableau: Created interactive dashboards and visualizations to present the findings and make the data insights easily accessible to stakeholders.
